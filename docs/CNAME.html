"About Me"

I graduated from Ridgewood High School in 2014 and was accepted into the Triton College Scholars Program.
In 2016, I finished my education at Triton and graduated with my associates degree. I then began to attend NEIU in the fall semester of 2016 to pursue my bachelors in CS.
When looking through the CS electives, Web Development appealed to me because I wanted to learn how to make websites effectively and the practicality is a bonus.
While I have only been here for two semesters, I currently have a 4.0 GPA and hope to maintain it!
I am planning on graduating from NEIU in December of 2018.

I remember that I always wanted to be a veterinarian when I was young, but that dream slowly died down over time.
I began playing video games at a young age and my passion for animals eventually turned into a passion for video games.
Because of this, I decided that I wanted to make video games, not just play them. This became the driving force behind my decision to pursue a degree in computer science.
While I think I would be happy with a wide array of jobs in the field, my end goal is to have a job creating video games.
Throughout my experiences with programming so far, if it ain't broke, don't fix it has become my go-to proverb.

HTML Terms Defined

Element
A pair of HTML tags that contains content.
Attribute
Code added to an element to provide more information about said element.
Document Type Definition (DTD)
Contains all of the attributes, elements, and syntax rules of the markup language that is being used.
Uniform Resource Locator (URL)
Connects the user to the appropriate web server and requests the specified file.
World Wide Web Consortium (W3C)
An organization that sets the standards for markup languages.
